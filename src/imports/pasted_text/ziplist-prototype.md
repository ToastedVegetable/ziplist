Create a polished desktop website prototype for an app called “ziplist.”

ziplist helps stressed Northwestern students decide what to eat with less mental effort. The main problem is that students are already mentally drained by homework, classes, schedules, and daily decisions, so deciding what to eat can feel overwhelming. The app should reduce decision fatigue by generating realistic meal options and a grocery list based on the student’s actual situation.

Overall design style:
Use a modern, friendly, student-centered design. The website should feel calm, simple, and low-effort. Use Northwestern-inspired purple accents, a soft light background, rounded cards, large readable text, and clear buttons. Avoid clutter. The design should feel like: “I’m tired, just help me pick food quickly.”

Use a clean desktop web app layout with strong spacing, accessible contrast, and simple navigation. Make the interface easy to scan and not overwhelming.

App title:
ziplist

Main tagline:
“Decide what to eat without overthinking it.”

Create these main pages/screens:

1. Homepage

The homepage should have the title “ziplist” at the top and the tagline:
“Decide what to eat without overthinking it.”

Below that, show three large option cards:

Card 1: “Browse Recipes”
Description: “Get realistic meal ideas based on your budget, diet, preferences, and cooking access.”
Button: “Plan My Meals”

Card 2: “Random Choice”
Description: “Let ziplist choose a meal for you when you do not want to decide.”
Button: “Spin the Wheel”

Card 3: “Upload Recipe”
Description: “Save your own recipes and add them to your weekly meal options.”
Button: “Add Recipe”

Each card should include a simple image placeholder or icon. The homepage should feel like a quick starting point, not a complicated dashboard.

2. Personalized Browse Recipes / Weekly Meal Plan Page

This page should not be a generic recipe library. Instead, it should start with an auto-generated personalized recipe list based on the student’s realistic situation.

At the top, include:
- Back button
- Heading: “Personalize Your Meals”
- Short description: “Tell ziplist a little about your week, and we’ll suggest meals and a grocery list that actually fit your life.”

Create a simple setup card or form with low-effort inputs:
- Weekly food budget or income range
- Dietary restrictions, such as vegetarian, vegan, halal, gluten-free, dairy-free, nut allergy
- Food preferences, such as high protein, quick meals, comfort food, healthy, cheap, easy prep
- Cooking access, such as microwave only, dorm kitchen, full kitchen, no cooking
- Number of meals needed for the week
- Ingredients the user already has

Use simple chips, dropdowns, sliders, and checkboxes so the user does not need to type too much.

Include a main button:
“Generate My Meals”

After the user generates meals, show a section titled:
“Your meals for the week”

Add a short note:
“Generated based on your budget, preferences, diet, and cooking access.”

Show recipe cards with realistic college-student meals. Each recipe card should include:
- Meal name
- Image placeholder
- Estimated cost
- Prep time
- Dietary/preference tags, such as quick, cheap, high protein, vegetarian
- Difficulty level
- Checkbox to keep or remove the meal

Use realistic placeholder recipe names such as:
- Chicken rice bowl
- Pesto pasta
- Egg sandwich
- Tofu stir fry
- Turkey wrap
- Ramen with vegetables
- Greek yogurt bowl
- Salmon rice bowl
- Black bean quesadilla
- Overnight oats

Include buttons:
- “Regenerate Meals”
- “Swap This Meal”
- “Make Grocery List”

The recipe page should also have filters/search options:
- Search by name
- Tag filters for breakfast, lunch, dinner, quick, cheap, high protein, vegetarian
- A “Wheel Mode” toggle or button

When a recipe is selected, show a detail panel with:
- Recipe name
- Larger image placeholder
- Ingredients
- Steps
- Estimated cost
- Prep time
- Tags
- Button: “Add to Grocery List”

3. Grocery List Section / Page

After the user accepts the generated meals, show a grocery list titled:
“Your grocery list for the week”

This grocery list should be based on the selected recipes.

The grocery list should:
- Group items by category, such as produce, protein, grains, dairy, frozen, pantry, and snacks
- Combine repeated ingredients across meals
- Show quantity
- Show estimated cost for each item
- Let users check off items
- Highlight items the user may already have
- Show a total estimated grocery cost at the bottom

Include buttons:
- “Swap Meal”
- “Regenerate Grocery List”
- “Save List”
- “Show Meal Summary”

The grocery list should feel practical and realistic for a Northwestern student shopping for the week. It should make the student feel like they do not need to plan everything manually.

4. Random Wheel Page

This page is for students who feel too overwhelmed to choose.

Include:
- Back button
- Heading: “Let ziplist choose”
- Short description: “Pick from your generated meals or create a quick wheel.”

Show a large meal spinner wheel in the center with 6 sections. Add a pin at the top of the wheel showing where the selected meal lands.

Below the wheel, include a large button:
“Spin”

On the left side, include a panel titled:
“Make Wheel”

The panel should include:
- Number of meal options, such as 4, 5, or 6
- Checkboxes or chips for categories like protein, balanced, vegetarian, quick, comfort food, breakfast, lunch, dinner
- Option to use meals from “Your meals for the week”
- Button: “Generate Wheel”

After spinning, show a result card:
“Tonight’s pick: [dish name]”

Include buttons:
- “Add to My List”
- “See Recipe”
- “Spin Again”

The wheel page should feel playful but not childish. It should reduce decision fatigue by making the choice for the student.

5. Upload Recipe Page

Create a simple upload page for users who want to save their own meals.

Include:
- Back button
- Heading: “Upload a Recipe”
- Short description: “Add a recipe you already like, and ziplist can include it in future meal plans.”

Create a simple form with:
- Recipe name
- Ingredients
- Steps
- Tags
- Estimated cost
- Prep time
- Image upload placeholder

Include buttons:
- “Preview”
- “Upload”

Keep this page simple and low-pressure.

Interaction flow:
The user starts on the homepage. They can choose one of three paths:
1. Browse Recipes / Plan My Meals
2. Random Choice / Spin the Wheel
3. Upload Recipe

The main intended flow is:
Homepage → Personalize Your Meals → Generated Weekly Meals → Grocery List → Optional Wheel Mode

The design should emphasize:
- Low cognitive load
- Fewer choices
- Realistic meals based on budget and lifestyle
- Quick decision-making
- A grocery list that makes the meals actionable
- A calm experience for stressed Northwestern students

Make the final design look like a polished desktop web app prototype with clear spacing, rounded cards, simple navigation, and a calm Northwestern-inspired color palette.
